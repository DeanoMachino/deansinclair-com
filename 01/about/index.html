Bio

Who are you?

I’m Dean Sinclair, a 20-year-old third year Game Development Student from Scotland, currently studying at Abertay University.

What do you do?

I make games. I'm a computer games programmer/developer.

No seriously, what do you ACTUALLY do?

I’ll be the first person to admit I’ve not made that many games (currently only like 2 besides university projects), but I’m still learning and that’s my aim.

Besides work, I obviously like to play a lot of games myself. A few favourites currently are Rocket League, The Binding of Isaac: Rebirth, Football Manager 2016, Crusader Kings II, and Europa Universalis IV. I mainly play PC games, but I have an Xbox 360 and PlayStation Vita too (of which I play neither regularly).

I also enjoy playing music, being a guitarist and bassist. Currently between bands but I used to do a lot of live performing.

What languages/software do you use?

In university it’s often C++ that I use, although I’ve dabbled in ActionScript 3.0, Java and Python. I’ve started to use C# more these days (in part due to a university group project using the MonoGame framework) but I’m still pretty new to the language.

I generally don’t use engines (we don’t have a good working relationship, see UDK 3.0 crashing ALL THE TIME) although I’m looking to gain some experience with them in the coming year — most likely with Unity.

What is this website for?

This site acts as both a development blog for anything I’m working on, and (hopefully in the near future) as a professional portfolio of all the work I’ve created. Think of it as a sort of “central hub”. I’ll be using this to explain what I’m currently up to dev-wise and also to keep me on track with my goals.

What are you currently working on?

I’ll update this in the near future, as soon as I get myself up-to-date with all the work I’ve done in 2015 and failed to blog about. Long story short, I’m working with a group of people in creating a top-down, multiplayer racing game for a large touchscreen device, all as part of a university project.
